Blinds Buddy

Dual-mode automated window blinds system integrating autonomous light-sensing and manual IR remote control.

Arduino SolidWorks 3D Printing Circuitry Embedded Systems
Download PDF

Fig. 1 — Electronics integrated into the 3D-printed enclosure during assembly, showing the LCD screen, passive buzzer, RGB LED, IR sensor, photoresistor (top left), and DC stepper motor (top right).

Objectives

  • Window blinds require constant manual adjustment to balance light and privacy throughout the day.
  • Design a system that responds to ambient light automatically, with optional IR remote override for manual control.

Outcomes & Contributions

  • Built a fully functional wall-mounted enclosure housing all electronics, 3D modeled and printed in-house.
  • Integrated dual-input control — photoresistors for autonomous mode and IR remote for manual — driving a DC stepper motor to position the blinds.
  • Delivered clear system feedback through an RGB LED, passive buzzer, and LCD screen.

Technical Details & Skills

  • Arduino programming for state-based motor control, photoresistor comparison logic, and IR signal parsing.
  • Circuitry design incorporating a stepper motor, photoresistors, IR receiver, passive buzzer, RGB LED, and LCD screen.
